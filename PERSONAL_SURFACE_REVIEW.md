# PERSONAL_SURFACE_REVIEW

## Current profile story

Chris Yoon currently reads as:

- a proven Senior Data Engineer with 10+ years in pipelines, validation, reconciliation, and data quality
- most recently a Senior Data Engineer at theScore / ESPN Bet with credible, metric-backed work
- a public builder using 1% Better.dev and GitHub as visible proof of recent execution
- someone who ships small products in public without claiming that small-product work is bigger than the core engineering story
- someone intentionally keeping poker separate from the recruiter-facing identity

Bottom line: the core story is good. The strongest version is "proven data engineer with current public shipping signal."

## What is working

- The data-engineering credibility is real, concrete, and easy to trust.
- theScore / ESPN Bet is the clearest anchor and gives the profile immediate legitimacy.
- The profile repeatedly signals that recent public work is there to support hiring trust, not replace experience.
- The best project choices support the main story well, especially Blue Jays Moneyball ETL.
- Poker is explicitly separated, which protects hiring clarity.
- The recruiter path is fast: README, resumes, site, and repos all connect cleanly.

## What is unclear

- The target role mix is still a little wide. "Senior Data Engineer," "Analytics Engineer," "Data Platform Engineer," and "AI Product Engineer" together can make the reader pause.
- The default story is strongest for data engineering, but some copy tries to sell both "data engineer" and "AI product engineer" at the same time.
- "Public build," "trust layer," and "proof of work" are directionally right, but they can sound meta if repeated too often.
- 1% Better.dev makes sense after reading, but the brand still needs one plain-English sentence explaining why it matters to a hiring manager.

## What feels too broad or distracting

- Repeated explanation about what the brand is, what it is not, and what should not become the main character.
- Too much emphasis on the profile surface itself instead of the underlying work.
- The AI-product angle is credible as an adjacent lane, but it is not as strong as the data-engineering story yet.
- Multiple small products listed with similar weight can flatten the signal instead of sharpening it.
- Any mention of internal systems or operating-system language would be distracting on public surfaces.

## What should stay

- Data engineer first positioning.
- theScore / ESPN Bet as the main credibility anchor.
- Quantified reliability, observability, reconciliation, and compliance signals.
- 1% Better.dev as a current-execution layer.
- Blue Jays Moneyball ETL as the strongest public technical proof.
- One quiet line that poker is separate from the hiring story.

## What should be simplified

- Narrow the default public identity to one clear sentence: data engineer first, public builder second.
- Reduce the number of target roles shown at once.
- Use fewer meta phrases about the profile and more plain language about recent work.
- Give fewer projects top-billing.
- Keep the AI-product variant available, but do not let it become the default public read.

## Top 5 improvements for hiring clarity

1. Tighten the headline around the strongest truth.
   Example direction: "Senior Data Engineer with 10+ years building reliable pipelines, data-quality systems, and visible public proof of recent execution."

2. Narrow the visible role targets.
   Keep the default recruiter-facing list centered on `Senior Data Engineer`, `Data Platform Engineer`, and maybe one adjacent role. Let `AI Product Engineer` stay secondary unless that is the specific application lane.

3. Replace repeated meta language with plain recruiter language.
   Swap phrases like "trust layer" or "main character" for simpler wording such as "live products and repos that show how I work now."

4. Feature fewer projects more intentionally.
   Lead with three: theScore / ESPN Bet experience, Blue Jays Moneyball ETL, and one 1% Better product or site surface. Everything else can stay lower in the stack.

5. Keep poker separate without over-explaining it.
   One line is enough. Repeating the separation too much can accidentally keep poker in the reader's head.

## Smallest useful cleanup changes

- Make the README headline slightly more direct and less brand-explanatory.
- Shrink the "Open to" role list.
- Trim one or two meta bullets from "What You Should Know" or "Public Build Direction."
- Keep only the strongest three public proof items near the top.
- Preserve the AI-product resume, but keep the default public profile centered on data engineering.
